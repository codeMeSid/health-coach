# 5-Day Workout Plan — Full Gym, Fat Loss Focus

**Profile:** 28 yrs | 95kg → 78kg goal | Sedentary baseline, full gym access, 5 days/week

## The Belly Fat Thing — quick, honest note

Spot reduction isn't real — no amount of ab exercises burns fat specifically from your belly. What actually shrinks belly fat is *overall* fat loss: the calorie deficit from your meal plan, combined with consistent training. Core work below builds strength and stability (genuinely useful), but the fat loss itself comes from the whole package, not the crunches.

## How This Is Structured

- **Upper/Lower split, run twice**, plus one dedicated cardio+core day — good balance of muscle retention and fat loss for someone starting from sedentary.
- **Each muscle group trained ~2x/week** — better for both strength gains and recovery than doing everything once.
- **Start conservative.** You're 95kg and coming from sedentary — the goal for week 1-2 is learning the movements with control, not going heavy. Leave 2-3 reps "in the tank" on every set for the first 2 weeks.
- **Rest 60-90 sec** between sets unless noted.
- **Progression:** once you complete all sets at the top of the rep range with good form for 2 sessions in a row, add a small amount of weight (~2.5-5%) next session.

**Every day, gym or not:** aim for 8,000–10,000 steps. This single habit is one of the highest-leverage things you can do for fat loss at your starting point — low joint stress, adds up fast, and doesn't eat into recovery the way extra gym sessions would.

## Day 1 — Upper Body A

**Warm-up:** 5 min light cardio (treadmill/bike) + arm circles, band pull-aparts

1. Flat Barbell or Dumbbell Bench Press — 3 sets × 10-12
2. Lat Pulldown — 3 sets × 10-12
3. Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press — 3 sets × 10-12
4. Seated Cable Row — 3 sets × 10-12
5. Triceps Rope Pushdown — 2 sets × 12-15
6. Dumbbell Bicep Curl — 2 sets × 12-15
7. Plank — 3 sets × 20-30 sec hold

**Cool-down:** 5 min stretch (chest, shoulders, lats)

---

## Day 2 — Lower Body A

**Warm-up:** 5 min light cardio + leg swings, bodyweight squats × 10

1. Leg Press — 3 sets × 12-15
2. Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift — 3 sets × 10-12
3. Leg Curl (machine) — 3 sets × 12-15
4. Leg Extension (machine) — 2 sets × 12-15
5. Standing Calf Raise — 3 sets × 15
6. Cable Crunch — 3 sets × 15

**Cool-down:** 5 min stretch (quads, hamstrings, calves)

*Note: at your current weight, prioritize the Leg Press over heavy barbell squats for now — same quad/glute stimulus, much less spinal and knee loading while you build a base. Revisit barbell squats once you're 15-20kg lighter and more conditioned, if you want them.*

---

## Day 3 — Cardio + Core

**Warm-up:** 3-5 min easy pace

1. Incline Treadmill Walk or Stationary Cycling — 30-35 min at a pace where you could hold a conversation but not sing
2. Core circuit, 2-3 rounds:
   - Plank — 30 sec
   - Bicycle Crunches — 15/side
   - Dead Bug — 10/side
   - Russian Twist — 15/side

**Cool-down:** 5-10 min stretch/mobility

*Why incline walking over running: at 95kg, running regularly before you've built some fitness and lost some weight is a common way people end up with knee or shin issues. Incline walking burns comparably well with far less impact. Once you're lighter and fitter, add jogging intervals if you enjoy running.*

---

## Day 4 — Upper Body B

**Warm-up:** same as Day 1

1. Incline Dumbbell Press — 3 sets × 10-12
2. Single-Arm Dumbbell Row — 3 sets × 10-12/side
3. Lateral Raises — 3 sets × 12-15
4. Wide-Grip Lat Pulldown or Assisted Pull-up — 3 sets × 10-12
5. Dips (assisted if needed) or Close-Grip Bench Press — 2 sets × 10-12
6. Hammer Curl — 2 sets × 12-15
7. Side Plank — 2 sets × 20-30 sec/side

**Cool-down:** 5 min stretch

---

## Day 5 — Lower Body B

**Warm-up:** same as Day 2

1. Goblet Squat or Bulgarian Split Squat — 3 sets × 10-12
2. Hip Thrust — 3 sets × 12-15
3. Walking Lunges — 2 sets × 12/leg
4. Seated Calf Raise — 3 sets × 15
5. Hanging Knee Raise or Mountain Climbers — 3 sets × 15

**Cool-down:** 5 min stretch

## A Few Rules to Actually Stick To This

- **First 2 weeks = learning phase.** Light-moderate weight, focus entirely on form. This is the single biggest predictor of whether people stay injury-free and keep showing up.
- **Any sharp joint pain (not muscle fatigue) = stop that exercise, swap for an easier variation.** Knees and lower back are the ones to be careful with at your current weight — don't push through pain.
- **If a gym session gets missed, don't try to "make it up"** by doubling the next one. Just resume the schedule.
- **Track your lifts** (weight × reps) in your phone notes — even roughly. Seeing the numbers go up over 8-12 weeks is a big motivation boost, especially before the scale shows much movement.
- Weight fluctuates day to day from water/food/sleep — weigh in once a week, same day, same time, and track the trend, not the daily number.
