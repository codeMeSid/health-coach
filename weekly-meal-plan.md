## GERD-Friendly Rules Built Into This Plan

✅ 5 smaller meals/day instead of 3 large ones
✅ Last meal 2–3 hours before bed
✅ Minimal oil, nothing deep-fried
✅ No tomato-heavy gravies, citrus, mint, or excess garlic/onion (onion kept optional/minimal in the egg curry)
✅ No caffeine, carbonated drinks, or chocolate
✅ Eggs cooked plain/mild (boiled, light masala) — they're naturally gentle on digestion

Also worth doing: don't lie down right after eating, eat slowly, and get "severe" symptoms checked by a doctor at some point — diet helps a lot but doesn't replace ruling out things like H. pylori.

## Recipe Bank

### 🍳 BREAKFAST

**Paneer-Moong Dal Stuffed Dosa**
Serves 1 | 20 min | 480 cal | P: 23g · C: 60g · F: 16g

- Fresh dosa batter — 180g (store-bought or pre-fermented homemade)
- Paneer, crumbled — 60g
- Cooked yellow moong dal — 4 tbsp
- Cumin seeds — ¼ tsp
- Turmeric — ¼ tsp
- Oil — 1.5 tsp
- Coconut chutney (no mint) — 2 tbsp

1. Mix paneer, moong dal, cumin, turmeric, and a pinch of salt for the stuffing.
2. Heat a non-stick tawa, grease lightly with ¼ tsp oil.
3. Pour a ladle of batter, spread thin in a circular motion.
4. Once edges lift, place stuffing in the center, fold.
5. Cook 1–2 min till golden. Repeat for a second dosa.
6. Serve hot with coconut chutney.

*Tip: skip tamarind chutney (acidity trigger) — coconut only. No batter on hand? Instant rice-suji dosa mix works.*

**Vegetable-Paneer Daliya (Broken Wheat Porridge)**
Serves 1 | 15 min | 470 cal | P: 20g · C: 52g · F: 17g

- Broken wheat (daliya) — 50g
- Paneer, cubed — 50g
- Mixed vegetables (peas, carrot) — 50g
- Ghee — 1 tsp
- Cumin seeds — ¼ tsp
- Milk — 50ml
- Curd, side — 100g

1. Dry roast daliya 2 min till aromatic, set aside.
2. Heat ghee, splutter cumin, add vegetables, sauté 2 min.
3. Add daliya, 1.5 cups water, salt. Simmer 10 min till soft.
4. Stir in paneer cubes and milk in the last 2 min.
5. Serve warm with curd on the side.

*Tip: cooks well in bulk — makes good next-morning leftovers, just add a splash of milk when reheating.*

**Ragi/Oats Idli with Peanut Podi**
Serves 1 | 15 min (if batter's ready) | 455 cal | P: 16g · C: 56g · F: 19g

- Ragi or oats idli batter — 200g (4 idlis)
- Coconut chutney (no mint) — 2 tbsp
- Curd — 100g
- Roasted peanut podi (peanuts, cumin, mild chili, salt) — 2 tbsp

1. Steam idli batter in a greased idli mould, 10–12 min till a knife comes out clean.
2. Serve 4 idlis with chutney, curd, and a sprinkle of peanut podi on the side.

*Tip: make a big batch of peanut podi once a week — keeps for days, boosts protein on any idli/dosa day.*

**Masala Vegetable Omelette + Toast**
Serves 1 | 12 min | 405 cal | P: 24g · C: 30g · F: 22g

- Eggs — 3 whole
- Capsicum, finely chopped — 30g
- Carrot, grated — 10g
- Cumin powder, turmeric — pinch each
- Oil — 1 tsp
- Whole wheat bread — 2 slices, toasted

1. Whisk eggs with a pinch of salt, cumin, and turmeric.
2. Heat oil in a non-stick pan, add capsicum and carrot, sauté 1–2 min.
3. Pour whisked eggs over the vegetables, let set on medium-low heat.
4. Fold in half once mostly set, cook 1 more min.
5. Serve with toasted bread.

*Tip: keep onion out or minimal if it's a personal trigger — capsicum and carrot give plenty of flavor without it.*

### 🥤 SNACKS (Mid-Morning & Afternoon)

**Boiled Eggs**
Serves 1 | 12 min | 210 cal | P: 18g · C: 1.5g · F: 15g

- Eggs — 3 whole
- Salt, pepper (mild) — pinch
- Water — enough to submerge eggs

1. Place eggs in a saucepan, cover with water by 1 inch.
2. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, simmer 8–10 min for firm yolks (7 min for slightly soft).
3. Transfer to cold water immediately, sit 2 min, then peel.
4. Season with salt and pepper.

*Tip: boil a batch of 6–9 at once — they keep 4–5 days refrigerated, unpeeled — and this becomes a zero-cook grab-and-go snack for the rest of the week.*

**Buttermilk + Roasted Makhana**
Serves 1 | 5 min | 245 cal | P: 9g · C: 27g · F: 9g

- Buttermilk (chaas) — 250ml
- Makhana (fox nuts) — 30g
- Ghee — ½ tsp
- Roasted cumin powder, salt — pinch
- Almonds — 8–10 pieces

1. Dry roast makhana in ½ tsp ghee till crisp, 4–5 min.
2. Whisk buttermilk with roasted cumin and salt (no mint).
3. Serve together with a small handful of almonds.

**Sprouts Chaat**
Serves 1 | 5 min (if pre-sprouted) | 215 cal | P: 13g · C: 25g · F: 8g

- Mixed sprouts (moong + chana), boiled — 100g
- Cucumber, chopped — 50g
- Roasted peanuts — 15g
- Chaat masala, salt — pinch (skip if it contains amchur/citrus)

1. Boil sprouts 5 min till just tender, drain.
2. Toss with chopped cucumber, peanuts, and mild seasoning.

*Tip: sprout a big batch of moong/chana at home over 2 days (soak, drain, leave covered) — cheaper than store-bought.*

### 🍛 LUNCH

**Moong Dal, Rice & Lauki Sabzi**
Serves 1 | 25 min | 580 cal | P: 28g · C: 75g · F: 19g

- Yellow moong dal, cooked — 1 cup
- Steamed rice — ¾ cup
- Lauki (bottle gourd), diced — 100g
- Paneer, cubed — 40g
- Cumin, turmeric, hing — pinch each
- Oil — 1.5 tsp
- Curd, side — 100g
- Cucumber salad — small bowl

1. Cook moong dal with turmeric and hing till soft; temper with cumin in ½ tsp oil.
2. Sauté lauki in 1 tsp oil till tender, season lightly, no tomato.
3. Lightly pan-sear paneer cubes, add to the sabzi.
4. Plate rice, dal, sabzi, curd, and cucumber salad (skip lemon dressing — use a pinch of roasted cumin instead).

**Rajma, Rice & Bhindi Sabzi**
Serves 1 | 25 min (using pre-cooked rajma) | 505 cal | P: 21g · C: 82g · F: 10g

- Rajma (kidney beans), cooked — 1 cup
- Steamed rice — ¾ cup
- Bhindi (okra), sliced — 100g
- Cumin, ginger, mild spices — pinch
- Oil — 1.5 tsp
- Curd, side — 100g

1. Simmer cooked rajma with cumin, ginger, and a touch of turmeric — minimal to no tomato.
2. Sauté bhindi in 1 tsp oil on medium heat till just cooked.
3. Serve with rice and curd.

*Tip: pressure cook a big batch of rajma on Sunday, portion and freeze — cuts this to a 15 min meal on the day.*

**Chole, Roti & Salad**
Serves 1 | 20 min (using pre-cooked chole) | 445 cal | P: 22g · C: 72g · F: 8g

- Chole (chickpeas), cooked — 1 cup
- Whole wheat roti — 2 medium
- Cumin, coriander powder, mild spice — pinch
- Oil — 1 tsp
- Curd, side — 100g
- Cucumber-carrot salad — small bowl

1. Simmer chole with cumin, coriander powder, and a touch of oil — light on tomato, no citrus finish.
2. Serve with roti, curd, and salad.

### 🌙 DINNER

**Paneer Bhurji + Roti**
Serves 1 | 15 min | 440 cal | P: 25g · C: 37g · F: 22g

- Paneer, crumbled — 100g
- Capsicum, peas — 40g
- Cumin, turmeric, mild chili — pinch
- Oil — 1.5 tsp
- Whole wheat roti — 2 medium

1. Heat oil, sauté capsicum and peas 2 min.
2. Add crumbled paneer, cumin, turmeric — cook 3–4 min. Minimal to no tomato.
3. Serve hot with roti and a simple salad.

**Moong Dal Khichdi + Curd**
Serves 1 | 20 min | 410 cal | P: 17.5g · C: 61g · F: 10.5g

- Rice — ½ cup
- Split moong dal — ½ cup
- Cumin, turmeric, hing — pinch each
- Ghee — 1 tsp
- Curd, side — 150g

1. Wash rice and dal together, pressure cook with turmeric, hing, and 2 cups water — 3 whistles.
2. Temper with cumin in ghee, stir through.
3. Serve warm with curd.

**Tofu-Paneer Stir-Fry + Rice**
Serves 1 | 15 min | 425 cal | P: 21g · C: 38g · F: 20g

- Tofu, cubed — 100g
- Paneer, cubed — 50g
- Mixed vegetables (beans, carrot, capsicum) — 80g
- Oil — 1.5 tsp
- Cumin, mild pepper — pinch
- Steamed rice — ¾ cup

1. Pan-sear tofu and paneer cubes in 1 tsp oil till lightly golden.
2. Add vegetables, stir-fry 4–5 min on medium-high.
3. Season lightly, serve over rice.

**Palak Paneer + Roti**
Serves 1 | 20 min | 435 cal | P: 22g · C: 45g · F: 20g

- Spinach (palak), blanched & pureed — 150g
- Paneer, cubed — 80g
- Cumin, ginger — pinch
- Oil — 1.5 tsp
- Whole wheat roti — 2 medium

1. Blanch spinach 2 min, blend to a smooth puree.
2. Heat oil, temper cumin and ginger, add spinach puree, simmer 5 min.
3. Add paneer cubes, simmer 2–3 min more. No cream, minimal salt.
4. Serve with roti.

**Egg Curry + Rice**
Serves 1 | 20 min | 455 cal | P: 23g · C: 38g · F: 23g

- Eggs — 3, boiled and peeled
- Curd — 50g
- Onion, finely chopped (optional, keep minimal) — 20g
- Cumin, turmeric, mild chili powder — pinch each
- Oil — 1.5 tsp
- Steamed rice — ¾ cup

1. Heat oil, sauté onion (if using) till soft, 2–3 min.
2. Add cumin, turmeric, chili powder, cook 30 sec.
3. Whisk in curd off-heat to avoid curdling, return to low heat, simmer 3–4 min into a light gravy.
4. Halve the boiled eggs, add to the gravy, simmer 2 min to warm through.
5. Serve over rice.

*Tip: curd-based gravy instead of tomato — gentler on acidity, still rich. Skip onion entirely if it bothers you; cumin and curd carry the flavor fine alone.*
